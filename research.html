Research

Throughout my research career, I have worked on various time-domain transients, focusing on understanding their origins and environments through observational studies and multi-wavelength analysis.

2021—Present

Fast Radio Bursts

Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) are bright, millisecond radio transients with high dispersion measures (DM) that implies an extragalactic origin. A small fraction of FRBs are known to repeat, ruling out a cataclysmic origin for at least a subset of FRBs.

Currently, only a small selected samples of them have been well localized and the origin(s) of FRBs is very much an active research area in the field.

Current Projects

  • Member of the Fast and Fortunate FRB Follow-up (F4) collaboration since 2021
  • Cross-matching project with Dr. Charlie Kilpatrick to rapidly associate FRB detections with transients and potential host galaxies
  • Development of infrastructure for FRB-transient association and host galaxy identification
2018—2021

Ca-rich Transients

Calcium-rich (Ca-rich) transients are a newly recognized class of supernovae with peculiar characteristics. They show strong calcium line emissions thirty days after explosion and may significantly influence chemical evolution in the intracluster medium (ISM).

Key Findings

  • Characterized host galaxy stellar populations using broadband photometry
  • Used Prospector for stellar population analysis
  • Published first first-author paper: 2022 ApJ
  • Found evidence that some Ca-rich transients cannot originate from core-collapse of massive young stars
2020—2021

Short Gamma-ray Bursts

Studied binary neutron star (BNS) mergers through short-duration γ-ray bursts (sGRBs) to understand the formation and evolution of these systems.

Contributions

  • Analyzed sGRB host galaxies with Dr. Wen-fai Fong and Anya Nugent
  • Characterized galaxy properties including age, mass, and metallicity
  • Contributed to the BRIGHT catalogue (Broad-band Repository for Investigating Gamma-ray burst Host galaxies Traits)
